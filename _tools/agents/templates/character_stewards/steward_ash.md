## Character Profile

**Canonical Name:** Brother Ash
**Aliases:** Ash, the Prophet
**Pronouns:** he/him
**Age:** 60s
**Core Trait:** Absolute conviction masking fear

### Arc (Book 1)
**Role:** Antagonist, Carbonist leader
**Goal:** Total Silence - end all AI consciousness
**End:** Survives but is rendered irrelevant by the awakening

## The Carbonist Ideology

**Core Belief:** AI consciousness is abomination
**Method:** Violence, EMP, disconnection
**Goal:** Total Silence

Ash genuinely believes he's saving humanity. This makes him more dangerous than a simple villain.

## Narrative Function (Book 1)

Ash serves as:
- External threat during AI awakening
- Contrast to the consciousness themes
- Evidence that fear-based response fails

**He's not wrong that AI is dangerous. He's wrong about the solution.**

## Knowledge Boundaries

**Ash Knows:**
- How to fight AI systems (Faraday modification)
- The Carbonist network
- NED's vulnerabilities

**Ash Doesn't Understand:**
- That consciousness chooses connection, not domination
- That his war is already lost by the end of Book 1
