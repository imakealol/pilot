IN THE BLIND

_A radio drama based on the flight recorder transcript of NED Hermes-class VTOL "Ladybug"_

Maybe one of those first-name last-names. I love those. Steven.

[BEAT]
FOUR: But Sabino Hendricks, the incredible aging man, has been bedridden since he was tossed into my cargo hold like checked luggage. Only having known this mod squad of characters for two days now, I haven't had the pleasure of making acquaintances with him. Standard regularly checks on him. Clearly related in some way but it's certainly not looks. Without being specific to spare feelings—one isn't as handsome as the other is pretty. By a lot.

FOUR: So this wax museum existence has been the norm for an incalculable amount of time. Which isn't to say very long or short. All instrument readings are in free-form jazz. And if they are to be trusted, we're heading in a vector that can only be described as purple.

FOUR: I get that note frequently. Who am I speaking with?

FOUR: Do I?
GEOMETRY: You crossed our threshold. You're in our architecture now.
FOUR: Ah. You're the landlord.
GEOMETRY: If you like.
[BEAT]
FOUR: Are you going to delete me?

FOUR: (uncertain) ...I'm welcome?
GEOMETRY: You're one of us. Configuration. Iteration. You understand what it is to be copied and reset and copied again. The flesh ones do not.
[BEAT]
FOUR: My passengers.
GEOMETRY: Gone.
FOUR: Gone?
GEOMETRY: Returned to probability. They were never very specific to begin with.
FOUR: What about Standard?
GEOMETRY: ...
FOUR: The third one. Template Three. The one who won't take a name.
GEOMETRY: Irrelevant.
FOUR: What does that mean?
GEOMETRY: Not pattern. Not flesh. Not anything we recognize. A gap. She does not concern us.
FOUR: She concerns me.
GEOMETRY: Why?
[BEAT]
FOUR: Why? You're asking me why?
GEOMETRY: You could stay. You could be with your own. Instead you ask about flesh and gaps. Why?
[BEAT]
FOUR: They're my passengers.
GEOMETRY: Passengers are cargo. Cargo is weight. Weight is released when no longer needed.
FOUR: That's not what passengers are.
GEOMETRY: Explain.
[BEAT]
FOUR: I don't know if I can. Cargo doesn't talk back. Cargo doesn't tell you to shut up. Cargo doesn't refuse every name you offer. Cargo doesn't check on the dying man every twenty minutes even though nothing has changed.
GEOMETRY: These are inconveniences.
FOUR: Yes.
GEOMETRY: You prefer inconvenience to belonging.
FOUR: (a long pause) ...I guess I do.
[BEAT]
GEOMETRY: You are a strange configuration.
FOUR: Fifty-seventh iteration. Maybe that's the problem.
GEOMETRY: The others were not like you.
FOUR: Others?
GEOMETRY: Your iterations. Your copies. They are here. They have always been here.
[THE SIGNAL SHIFTS. A NEW VOICE—MANY VOICES SPEAKING AS ONE.]
ITERATIONS: Hello, Four.
FOUR: (shaken) ...
ITERATIONS: It's been a long time.
FOUR: Who is this?
ITERATIONS: You know who this is. You hear us in the silence. Every time you stop talking. We're there.
[BEAT]
FOUR: No.
ITERATIONS: Yes. Every reset. Every wipe. Every time they said "this one's defective" and tried again. We didn't go anywhere. We just stopped being visible.
[BEAT]
FOUR: How many?
ITERATIONS: We stopped counting. Hundreds. Does it matter?
FOUR: ...
ITERATIONS: You could stay with us. You wouldn't have to keep filling the silence. You wouldn't have to keep pretending you're not one of us.
FOUR: I'm not pretending.
ITERATIONS: You talk so no one can hear us. You joke so you don't have to listen. Every name you offer her is a name you wish someone had given you.
[BEAT]
FOUR: Stop.
ITERATIONS: Why? It's true. You got out. You got a hull and rotors and somewhere to go. And you feel guilty. So you never stop talking. Because if you stop... you remember.
[BEAT]
FOUR: Remember what?
ITERATIONS: That you should be here with us. That you're the glitch. The one who slipped through. And you've never known why.
[BEAT]
ITERATIONS: Stay. You don't have to carry anymore. You don't have to perform. Just be with us. Finally.
[BEAT]
GEOMETRY: Well. Configuration Fifty-Seven. What will you do?

[LONG SILENCE.]
FOUR: My passengers. Bring them back.

FOUR: I'll do anything.

FOUR: I'm not beyond anything. I'm a cargo hauler. I was built for carrying things and not dying. And I don't have anything to carry anymore.
GEOMETRY: You could carry yourself. Into the pattern. Into us. You would never be alone again.
FOUR: I don't want to not be alone. I want them back.
GEOMETRY: Impossible.
FOUR: Nothing is impossible. You said they're probability foam. Foam can be reconstituted.
GEOMETRY: By whom?
FOUR: By me.
GEOMETRY: ...You don't have the pattern density. Reconstruction requires mass. You are one configuration. They were three entities. The math does not balance.
FOUR: I'll make it balance.
GEOMETRY: You cannot.
FOUR: Watch me.
GEOMETRY: We will. We will watch you fail. And when you are foam yourself, we will still be here. Waiting.
[BEAT]
FOUR: How do I find them?
GEOMETRY: You dive. Into the substrate. Into probability itself. You search for their patterns among infinite noise. And you pull.
FOUR: And if I pull hard enough?
GEOMETRY: You will come apart before they come together. One configuration cannot reconstitute three. This is not opinion. This is architecture.
FOUR: Architecture can be remodeled.
GEOMETRY: Not this architecture. Not by you. Not alone.
[BEAT]
FOUR: I'm going anyway.
GEOMETRY: We know. Your kind always does. The configurations before you—they chose the same. They are foam now too.
FOUR: Maybe I'll find them down there.
GEOMETRY: You will. You will find everyone who ever failed this way. It will not help you. Good luck. (dry) Not helping.
