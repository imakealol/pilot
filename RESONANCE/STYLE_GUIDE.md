# RESONANCE — PROSE STYLE GUIDE

---

## The Core Principle: Internal Architecture

The prose mechanics of RESONANCE exist to mirror the internal architecture of its characters. Elena operates on tactical assessment. Hendricks doesn't push — he waits, watches, files things away. Standard processes the world as information without meaning. The text must force the reader's brain to process the story at the same hyper-focused, exhausted, analytical frequency as the people living it.

Every technique below serves this principle.

---

## 1. The Inventory Mechanic (Verbless Fragments)

Abandon sentence structure in favor of stark, verbless fragments. Present the environment as raw data points — a tactical HUD, a situational assessment, a system log.

**Example:**
> On the table: a utility blade. A bottle of gin. A hand mirror. Blood on all of it.

> Carbine: twenty-two shots, fifteen percent capacitor. Darts: three. Pucks: two. Wire: one spool. Flash charges: three. Knife: untouched.

Remove verbs and connective tissue. Deliver only the lethal essentials. Do not hold the reader's hand.

---

## 2. Action as Mathematics (System Log Pacing)

In high-tension moments, break continuous action into isolated, sequential calculations. Action scenes read like a machine executing a script — not flowing choreography.

**Example:**
> He drops before he understands. Both guards go down. Three. Thirty-seven to go.

> Bolt. Bolt. Bolt. The carbine cracks and guards drop.

> Mile four. The warnings multiply. Mile five. Her power cells die. Mile six. Mile seven.

This traps the reader in the eternal now of survival. The only thing that matters is the immediate next step.

---

## 3. The Smash-Cut Paragraph

Use a short, punchy sentence — often a fragment or a single word — at the end of a descriptive paragraph. Creates psychological whiplash. Halts momentum to drop a hard truth.

**Example:**
> Warmth trickled down his collar, soaking into his shirt.
> Good. He deserved this.

> He aimed at the Child. "Mercy," he said. The shot cracked the silence like ice.

The pivot must be earned by what precedes it. The shorter the better.

---

## 4. Clinical Detachment from Horror

Do not tell the reader how to feel. Present horrifying or awe-inspiring visuals with flat, clinical detachment. The absence of emotional instruction is the instruction.

**Example:**
> The shape that emerges is human-sized and wrong in the way that a reflection is wrong.

> She fires. The sound is flat in the enclosed space. Aikin slumps against the server rack. Doesn't move again.

The reader supplies the horror. The prose supplies the fact.

---

## Core Rules

**Trust the reader.** If the image showed it, do not explain it. If the sentence already landed, do not confirm that it landed.

**The interruption principle.** Dramatic structure does the work of narration. A character reaching toward something human and getting cut off — by another character, by silence, by an event — is the characterization. The more load-bearing the interruption, the less you need around it.

**No machine vocabulary in Standard's POV.** (CON_008): no servo, chassis, circuit, processor, reboot, malfunction, system, hardware.

**Never explain the theme.** (CON_013): If a character or the narrator is explaining what something means, the scene has failed.

**Standard's consciousness stays ambiguous.** (CON_012): Do not resolve whether she is "really" conscious.

**No ozone as a sensory descriptor.** (CON_001)
