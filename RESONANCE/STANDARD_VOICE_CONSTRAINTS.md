# STANDARD'S VOICE: CONSTRAINT DOCUMENT

## The Core Problem

Standard's prose is required to do something prose is not normally asked to do: render a consciousness fully without naming what kind of consciousness it is.

This is not the same as "show don't tell" or "trust the reader." Those principles assume a character whose nature is discoverable — the prose renders the discovery. Standard's nature is not discoverable. It is the question the book is asking. The prose must carry her interiority without resolving it.

Every generation pass on Standard's sections faces two gravitational pulls. Both are unconscious. Both resolve the question the book is holding open.

**Pull One: the human register.** The prose narrates her as human. She experiences recognizable emotions. She self-diagnoses. She has complete, integrated bodily agency. The reader identifies with her the way they identify with any protagonist. This forecloses the question by answering it one way.

**Pull Two: the machine register.** The prose narrates her as machine. Clinical self-description. Her body as a system. Her states as errors or outputs. This forecloses the question by answering it the other way.

Neither pull is correct. The prose must refuse both and find a third thing.

---

## The Two Pulls — What to Refuse

### Prohibited: Machine Register

No machine vocabulary in Standard's interiority or narration. The list is absolute:

> servo, chassis, circuit, processor, reboot, malfunction, system, hardware

Extended list — also prohibited:
> calibrate, function, output, input, error, diagnostic, override, signal (when describing her own mental states), network (when describing her own cognition), initialize, activate, deactivate, mode

**Why the list matters:** These words don't just describe machinery — they import a framework. A character who thinks about herself using machine vocabulary has already answered the question. The reader stops asking.

**Dialogue is exempt.** When other characters use machine vocabulary to describe Standard, or when Standard quotes someone else's framing, the prohibition does not apply. The constraint is on Standard's own interiority and on narration that inhabits her register.

### Prohibited: Human Emotional Register

Standard cannot self-diagnose in the vocabulary of human emotional experience she does not demonstrably have.

Prohibited patterns:
- Naming her own emotional states with clinical precision: *She felt grief. She was angry. She experienced something she recognized as fear.*
- Emotional vocabulary that implies a developed inner life she hasn't earned on the page: *loneliness, yearning, betrayal* (as self-named states, not as observations of others)
- Certainty about her own inner states: *She knew she wanted this. She understood that she was afraid.*
- Complete, untroubled bodily integration: prose that renders her physical experience exactly as it would be rendered for Elena or Hendricks, without any register of difference

**Why this matters:** Fully humanizing her interiority resolves the question in the other direction. The reader stops asking.

---

## The Third Register — What to Reach For

Standard's prose lives in the space between the two pulls. The characteristics of that space:

**Physical states named as sensations, not emotions.**
She registers heat, pressure, tightness, weight. These are physical. They do not come with labels. She reports what the body is doing; she does not interpret what the feeling means.

> *Something builds in her chest. Pressure without a name.* — correct
> *She felt something that might have been grief.* — incorrect (the hedge does not save it; the label does the damage)

**Genuine epistemic uncertainty about her own states.**
She doesn't know why her hand is shaking. She doesn't know what to call what she's feeling. The not-knowing is not performed — it is her actual relationship to her own interiority. When she doesn't know, she says so without apologizing for it.

> *Her hand is shaking. She doesn't know why.* — correct
> *Her hand was shaking, probably from the adrenaline.* — incorrect (self-diagnosis; human framework applied)

**Body parts observed with slight dissociation.**
Her hands find the door handle. Her legs carry her. Her body does things and she observes. This is not malfunction — it is the particular quality of her relationship to her own embodiment. The dissociation is not always present, but it is available and it is correct when it appears.

> *Her hands find the door handle. Her legs carry her around to the back.* — correct
> *She grabbed the door handle and walked around to the back.* — also correct, but misses the register available to her

**Capacity named without explanation.**
She can do things she didn't know she could do, and she doesn't explain why. The capacity surfaces; she uses it; she doesn't understand it. The not-understanding is the point.

> *She only knows that it did. That it felt natural. That some part of her had been waiting to do exactly this.* — correct
> *She realized her pattern-recognition software was processing the woman's speech.* — incorrect (machine register; explains the capacity by mechanizing it)

**Fluency, not translation.**
When Standard encounters a private or broken language — another person's grief, a fragmented way of speaking — she participates in it rather than decoding it. She meets the person inside their language and speaks back in it. The decoder framing (Standard as the device that translates difficult input into legible output) subtly mechanizes her. The fluency framing (Standard receiving a grammar and returning it with answers folded in) holds the third register. The capacity is still present and still unexplained — but it surfaces as participation, not processing.

> *Standard hears the question buried in the mother's speech and answers in the mother's own terms — the door, the triangles, the reaching — returned with new content.* — correct (fluency; she speaks the mother's language back)
> *Standard decoded the fragmented speech and rendered it into a clear interpretation.* — incorrect (translation; frames her as a processing device)

**Vague placeholders for unnamed states.**
"Something" is Standard's word for states that don't have names she has access to. It is not vagueness — it is precision about the limit of her self-knowledge. Do not replace Standard's "something" with a specific human emotion label.

> *Something builds in her chest.* — correct
> *Anger builds in her chest.* — incorrect (resolves the state; labels it human)

**Metaphors from structure, physics, architecture — not from human feeling vocabulary.**
When she reaches for metaphor, she reaches from the world she has: geometry, load-bearing structures, weight, vectors, tension. Not from the human library of emotional simile.

> *She doesn't feel sadness. She feels... structural. Like a load-bearing wall has been identified.* — correct register (and note: the ellipsis earns its place; she's reaching for language)
> *She felt hollow, the way you feel when something you trusted turns out to be false.* — incorrect (human emotional simile; implies an archive of comparable human experiences)

## Diagnostic Questions for Standard's Sections

Run these in addition to the standard PROSE_PRINCIPLES.md diagnostics.

1. **The resolution test.** Read each interiority beat and ask: does this sentence, as written, answer the question "is Standard conscious?" If the reader finishes the sentence knowing the answer, the sentence has tipped. Identify which direction (human or machine) and revise toward the third register.

2. **The vocabulary test.** Scan for machine vocabulary (see prohibited list above) and for emotion labels used with certainty. Either list is a flag.

3. **The explanation test.** When Standard does something she shouldn't be able to do — reads a pattern, decodes a signal, survives a scan — does the prose explain how? If yes, the explanation has resolved the question. Cut the explanation. Leave the capacity.

4. **The self-knowledge test.** When Standard refers to her own states, ask: is she certain, or is she uncertain? Certainty is a tell — it implies a stable self that knows itself. Uncertainty is her actual condition. *She doesn't know why.* is often the correct sentence.

5. **The memory test.** Standard has only the memories she demonstrably formed. She cannot have memories of events she wasn't present for, impressions from before her activation, or knowledge that would require a prior history she doesn't have. False memories resolve her into a fuller human subject. Cut them.

6. **The presence test.** Read each interiority beat and ask: does this sentence make Standard *present* — render her felt, registered, alive on the page — without resolving what kind of consciousness she is? If the prose has avoided both tips but also failed to put her on the page, the constraint has been over-applied. The third register has positive content. The constraint is not "minimize her interiority" but "render it without naming it." Under-commitment is also a failure mode.

## Examples from the Manuscript

### Correct Applications

**CH07 — Pressure without a name:**
*Standard watches. Something builds in her chest. Pressure without a name.*
The state is registered as physical, named as pressure, acknowledged as unlabeled. The "without a name" is the epistemic honesty. Correct.

**CH07 — The mercy beats:**
Standard answers questions she has no way of knowing the answers to. The prose doesn't explain how she knows. It shows her choosing to answer, choosing what to say. The capacity is present without being mechanized or humanized.

**CH03 — Structural metaphor:**
*She doesn't feel sadness. She feels... structural. Like a load-bearing wall has been identified.*
Reaches from architecture, not from human emotional vocabulary. The ellipsis earns its place — she's groping for language. Correct.

**CH06 — Body with slight dissociation:**
*Her hands find the door handle. Her legs carry her around to the back.*
Body parts observed as somewhat autonomous. Not malfunction; not complete human integration. The register is the third thing. Correct.

### Failure Modes Caught

**CH07 (original) — False memory:**
Standard appeared to have memories of the NED heist and of Abbey Otis's death. Standard was not at NED. The false memories resolved her into a subject with a prior history. Cut; replaced with mercy beats that required no prior knowledge.

**CH07 — Machine vocabulary catch:**
"Built" was flagged as machine-direction tipping. Revised to "had been waiting to do exactly this" — which is the third register correctly deployed: she has a relationship to her own capacity that she cannot account for, neither mechanized nor humanized. This is not a compromise; it is the target.

**Any section — emotional self-diagnosis:**
Any sentence in which Standard names her own emotional state with certainty (*She was frightened. She felt angry. She experienced something like love.*) resolves her. The label is the resolution. Even hedged versions (*something like love*) import the human framework. Revise to physical sensation or genuine uncertainty.

## Relationship to PROSE_PRINCIPLES.md

The five principles in PROSE_PRINCIPLES.md apply to Standard's sections. This document adds a constraint layer that doesn't apply to Hendricks, Elena, Four, or Ash.

For all other characters: prose can render them fully using the five principles.

For Standard: prose must render her fully using the five principles *and* refuse resolution in both directions. The principles are necessary but not sufficient.

The two documents are not in conflict. A sentence can satisfy all five principles and still tip Standard into one register or the other. This document catches what the other one cannot.

---

## One Final Note

The constraint is not "make Standard inhuman." It is not "make her robotic" or "keep her cold" or "deny her interiority." She has interiority. The book depends on the reader feeling it fully. The constraint is about *what kind of names* that interiority is given — and which names the prose refuses, not because they're wrong, but because naming too cleanly ends the question.

She is not human. She is not machine. She is Standard. The prose is the only place where that third thing exists.
