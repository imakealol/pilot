# VOICE PRINCIPLES: THE ROUGHENING LAYER

## Purpose

PROSE_PRINCIPLES.md and STANDARD_VOICE_CONSTRAINTS.md are *smoothing* instruments — they remove throat-clearing, label-telling, machine vocabulary, and over-application. This document is the opposite axis. It describes the **motivated irregularity** that makes prose read as a person rather than as a machine-smooth default: voice, idiosyncrasy, cadence, and the occasional deliberate rule-break.

**This document overrides the smoothing instruments where they conflict — but only for the features named here, and only when those features are applied as a system.** A close-read or enforcer pass must treat the patterns below as *intentional and protected*, not as violations to correct. If a future pass "fixes" a runaway sentence or a register collision that this document sanctions, that is the regression.

The model exemplar is **CH1 (Rude Awakening)** — the manuscript's own opening, which is rougher, more interior, more bodily, and more tonally raw than the chapters that follow. We are not importing a foreign style; we are propagating CH1's native texture into the flatter chapters.

---

## The Governing Principle: System, Not Spot-Weld

Roughness reads as voice only when it is **consistent within a unit and motivated by the character's state.** CH1 works because the *whole chapter* commits to it. A single runaway sentence dropped into an otherwise-polished chapter reads as a stray — and a sharp editor (human or AI) will strip it. The fix is to make the character's cognitive/verbal signature run **throughout** each of their chapters, not spot-welded into one paragraph.

This is also what makes it durable: a one-off slip reads as a mistake; a patterned choice reads as a decision. We are building **systems**, not scattering noise.

**Hard guardrail:** none of this means typos, comma errors, or random tense slips. Those read as an unproofed draft, not as authorship, and they are the first thing any editor removes. Every smudge below is a *motivated choice with a reason behind it.*

## The Earned-by-Contrast Rule (governs all tic-rationing)

No device in this document or in `RESONANCE_prose_texture_analysis.md` is a sin in itself — not the triad, the fragment-punch, the "not X, but Y" antithesis, the scene-button, the em-dash apposition. Each is a legitimate tool. The defect is only ever **overuse, uniformity, or reflexive application** — the device deployed as a default rather than reserved for the moment that earns it. "Ration" therefore never means "purge to a quota." It means **restore contrast** so the device lands when it is wanted.

A device earns its place when **both** hold:
- **Contrast** — it stands out from the cadence around it. If every paragraph triads (or buttons, or fragments), none of them land.
- **Motivation** — the moment wants it: gravity, ritual, finality, escalation, or a structural job the device uniquely does.

**Worked example — triadic description.** A triad is *required or permitted* when the moment carries weight and the three-ness does structural work: the three elements **escalate, accumulate, or reduce**, so the "three" is load-bearing rather than decorative (e.g. CH11's "walls drawn inward to a line, the line to a point, the point to nothing" — the triad *enacts* the collapse it names; or an opening that widens "nobody came back for his things / nobody came back for anything" to establish a dead world). A triad is a tic to vary or recast only when it is reflexive at a low-stakes beat, its three elements are interchangeable (two or four would lose nothing), or the surrounding prose is already triad-saturated.

**Separate guard:** an earned triad — or any construction — must not be **reused verbatim** in nearby passages. That reuse, not the triad, is the self-similarity tell (CH11 reuses "line to a point, point to nothing" twice).

**The test for any flagged tic:** does the device do work *here*, and stand out from its surroundings — or is it the default cadence? Keep the first; vary the second. Quota-cutting the earned instances is itself a failure mode, the over-correction twin of the monotony it is trying to fix.

**Negation is not the target; the marked construction is.** What this rations is the conspicuous, repeated antithesis *form* — "Not X. Y." / "not X, but Y" — not negation as meaning. Negation folded into ordinary syntax ("rising," against an implied "settling"; "move against the air") is invisible to the C3 count and is fine — it is often exactly where negation should live. Converting a marked form to an unmarked one keeps the meaning and sheds the tell. Do not hunt negation to zero; that flattens the prose and mistakes the proxy for the target. **But the marked _construction_ itself is never used — there is no earned exception for it.** Always phrase the negation another way ("Not a hand. A pressure." → "a pressure where a hand should be"; "Not toward movement—toward sound" → "toward the only thing still making noise"). The meaning survives in unmarked syntax; the construction does not.

**Density — spike, don't sustain (the editorializing is subject to this rule too; CH2 pilot lesson).** The Track B voice itself obeys Earned-by-Contrast. Voice intrusions and aphoristic asides must **concentrate at the beats that earn them** — emotion, betrayal, death, decision — and **recede in action and transition.** An attitude-tag or a clever button on *every* beat is its own metronome: it trades camera-eye monotony for aphorism monotony, and the reader starts anticipating the wry closer the way they'd anticipate a fragment-punch. The POV character's voice should be unmistakable where it lands and nearly invisible where the scene is just moving pieces. A rewrite that editorializes uniformly has not found the voice; it has found a new default.

## Relationship to the Other Documents

- **PROSE_PRINCIPLES.md** still governs baseline craft (rhythm, fragments, silence, show-don't-tell, affirmative). It is necessary but no longer sufficient, and its do-not addenda yield to this document's sanctioned features.
- **STANDARD_VOICE_CONSTRAINTS.md** still governs *what register* Standard occupies (no machine vocab, no emotion self-diagnosis, no resolving her consciousness, no false memory). This document governs *how that register sounds and moves on the page.* The two stack: Standard's voice = third register (constraints) + the cadence below (Track A).

## TWO TRACKS

Every chapter has an assigned POV (see CHAPTERS.yaml — note the title↔file numbering offset; brief by title and path, never by YAML number). The roughening splits by who holds the POV.

- **Track A — Standard.** Third register, **no editorializing.** Her interiority is roughened *within* the constraint (it cannot acquire opinion, judgment, or certainty about her own states).
- **Track B — Editorializing characters** (Elena, Hendricks, Ash, Four, Dante). The narration may take on the character's attitude, opinion, and voice — including being wrong on purpose.
- **Exempt:** the epilogue ("no narrator, pure imagery") and any "Omniscient" sections. Multi/intercut-POV chapters are segmented by section before any voice pass.

## TRACK A — Standard's Cognitive Signature

Standard cannot editorialize. Her roughness comes from rendering a (possibly-synthetic) mind under load. Make these **ever-present** in her chapters, not occasional.

1. **Command-and-contradiction self-talk.** Her italics are *instructions she gives herself, and they fail and escalate* — not wry asides. CH1: *"Stop crying."* → "But she can't stop crying." → *"Stop crying!"* The voice orders; the body disobeys; the order sharpens. This is the single biggest differentiator from the later wry-quip italics. Make it the default mode of her interior voice.

2. **Count / procedure as thinking-rhythm.** When she is overwhelmed, the prose fragments into process and enumeration. CH1: *"In. Hold. Out. Hold. In. Fill chest and diaphragm. Hold. Out. Slow—4—5."* Standard already counts seconds when destabilized elsewhere; lean into it as a recurring cognitive tic — destabilization → the prose breaks into steps and numbers.

3. **Bodily, unliterary diction under stress.** When her state coarsens, the register coarsens. CH1: *"another part of her is telling it to shut the fuck up."* Drop the polish under duress — crude, blunt, plain. The later chapters keep her too composed.

4. **Runaway sentences that double back and break off.** At least one sentence per Standard chapter that overstays, self-interrupts, and trails into a dash. CH1: *"she feels, with her fingers—ruined now, the pads raw and weeping where skin has peeled back—but no less sensitive to the surface she begins to investigate, to search, to—"* This breaks the long-long-short metronome. (Dash as *rupture*, not smooth apposition.)

5. **Logic-as-coping.** When feeling threatens to overwhelm her, the prose pivots to cold problem-solving, and **the seam between the two is visible.** CH1: *"in order to thrive, one first needs to survive; to think, to solve the problem at hand."* The instinct to convert terror into a solvable problem is characterful for a possibly-synthetic mind — and it is genuinely ambiguous (human grit or machine instinct), which actively *holds* CON_012 open. **Boundary:** render it as *reasoning* (aphorism, deduction, the next logical step), never as *mechanism* ("calculating," "processing," "running diagnostics" — that trips CON_008 and resolves the question toward machine). Reason-as-coping, not mechanism-as-coping.

6. **Suppress the smooth tics inside her head.** In Standard POV specifically: ration the scene-button one-liner, cut the "not X, but Y" antithesis, and never let her italics become ironic quips. Her interiority should read *earlier-drafted and rougher* than the external narration around it.

**Two calibration rules (from the CH4 Track A pilot):**

- **Alternate the textures; don't repeat one device.** Pervasive is the target — but *identical* pervasive is its own metronome (the Earned-by-Contrast / spike-don't-sustain logic applies inside Track A too). CH1 alternates command (1) / count (2) / cold deduction (5). Don't run two adjacent high-load beats on the same device — e.g. two endurance beats both on italic imperatives ("hold… hold…"); the second diminishes the first. Vary by the cognitive *shape* of the moment: an acute involuntary flinch is command-driven; a sustained effort runs on count + reasoning. The **A5 reasoning seam is the prized connective** — surface it to break a run of imperatives (CH4 palm-squeeze: "the flinch is the answer the scanner wouldn't give him").

- **A3 (coarsening) scales with person and state.** CH1's profanity ("shut the fuck up") lives in *first-person hysteria*. In third-person, composed-under-load Standard, the same swear reads as Track B attitude, not coarsened register — there the ceiling is bodily-blunt monosyllables. Match the coarsening to POV-distance and her state.

**Exemplar to re-read before any Standard pass:** CH1, entire. It does 1–6 simultaneously, without a single opinion or self-diagnosis — proof that the third register can be visceral and rough without editorializing.

## TRACK B — Editorializing Characters (working sketch; calibrate on the CH2/Elena pilot)

For Elena, Hendricks, Ash, Four, Dante, the narration in their POV may carry their voice. Starting techniques (to be refined by the pilot):

- **Voice intrusion / attitude.** The narration editorializes, exaggerates, judges, or is wrong on purpose in the character's manner — a dry aside, an unreliable summary. (Hendricks: bitter economy. Four: wry. Ash: doctrinal certainty. Elena: controlled, code-switching.) This is the most "human" thing the camera-eye style currently suppresses, and it cannot be "corrected" out because removing it removes voice.
- **Register collisions.** Let a clinical sentence sit beside a crude or slangy one without smoothing the seam.
- **Sentence-length whiplash** (shared with Track A item 4): genuine runaway sentences, not just the tidy short-punch.
- **Dash as rupture** (shared): fracture, don't filigree.
- **Motivated tense as a system** (shared): present = now, past = remembered/reflective, applied consistently within its unit so it reads as architecture, not a slip. (CH18/CH20 backstory already do this; formalize it.)
- **Idiolect via the stewards.** Each character steward owns a verbal habit / pet word / repeated "wrong" grammar. This is the most durable smudge; the steward files are the place to record it.
- **Off-model specificity.** A few inert, non-symbolic concrete details (a brand, an exact mundane number) that don't pull thematic weight — lived observation, not generated texture.
- **Soften the buttons** (shared with Track A item 6): reserve the portent one-liner for genuine climaxes; let some scenes exit on an earned paragraph or trail off.

CON_013 still holds for Track B: a character may *opine* about events and people (voice), but may not *explain the book's theme* (failure). Opinion ≠ thesis.

## For the Apparatus (close-read, enforcer, stewards)

When reviewing a chapter that has had a voice pass:
- Do **not** flag the Track A/B features above as violations. A runaway sentence, a crude word under stress, a command-italic, a register collision, a motivated tense shift — these are protected.
- **Do** still flag: canon/continuity errors, blocking/plot drift, machine vocabulary in Standard's register (CON_008), resolution of Standard's consciousness (CON_012), theme-explaining (CON_013), and genuine sloppiness (typos, unmotivated slips).
- The test for any candidate "fix": does it remove a *motivated, systematic* voice feature (refuse the fix) or a *canon/continuity/sloppiness* defect (apply it)?

## Manuscript-Level Checks (the rubric and per-chapter passes CANNOT see these — and this layer *breeds* them)

The per-chapter close-read and the scoring rubric optimize locally and are **structurally blind to two emergent, cumulative properties.** Worse, the voice rollout actively *feeds* both — it propagates the structural-metaphor family and the capacity-surfacing reasoning seams across every Standard chapter. Run these as dedicated, human/lead-eyed, whole-manuscript passes; never trust the per-chapter gate to catch them.

1. **Motif-family frequency.** Track metaphor *families*, not instances: architecture/load-bearing, stone-in-chest, drawer/filing/shelf, tuning-fork. A family appearing in nearly every Standard chapter (1–2 each) has lost its scarcity — reads "polished" up close, "samey" across a full read — even though each instance passes locally. Census (grep), then thin to restore contrast. Earned-by-Contrast applies to motif *families*.
2. **Central-question vector (CON_012 at manuscript scale).** Inventory every "Standard mysteriously can do / knows things" beat; classify HOLDS (ambiguous) vs LEANS-SOURCED (implies a system/origin underneath). The danger is not any single beat — each can *ask* — but the **vector**: if they all lean the same way, the accumulation *tells* (she has latent functions) with no mechanism named. That is the G2 *spirit* at book scale. Rules: keep the vector **two-directional** (protect/seed counterweights — a capacity that *fails* to arrive, an answer *withheld*, an authority that *refuses to classify her*); thin the densest machine-spec beats; never let the prose **name its own pattern** ("the way they keep knowing things ahead of her"). The book may answer at its sanctioned endpoint (CH44 dissolution), but the pre-endpoint arc must still ASK to the threshold, not pre-deliver the mechanism (CH40 was pre-spending it — thinned Session 69).
