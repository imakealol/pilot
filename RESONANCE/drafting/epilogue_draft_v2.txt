THE BEDTIME STORY

---

She does it alone.

Late. The building empty. The compound small enough to hold between two fingers — Morton's formula, his name on the label, three years of her work in the compound, and she has never once considered taking it herself.

She does not consider it now. She simply does.

The needle finds the vein without ceremony. No record in the ledger. No witness.

She sets it down. The light above the bench hums at a frequency she has no name for yet.

---

*You have your father's face*, she tells her daughter. *But not his frequency.*

Elena is seven. She doesn't know what that means.

Neither does Lena, not entirely. Not yet.

---

The second century is lonelier than the first.

She learns to hold people loosely. She loves them anyway.

---

A form. A new signature. Tuesday, no ceremony.

Marisol.

She doesn't explain this to anyone.

---

She holds Elena the day she is born and understands, for the first time in a hundred and forty years, what it means to be afraid of time.

---

Morton, across the table. Both of them not aging. The particular silence of two people who share a secret they will never name.

She keeps his. He keeps hers. That is the arrangement.

---

Ash's hands. The warmth of them. The way he held things as though they might break.

They did.

---

The body leaves quietly.

She doesn't watch it go. She is already everywhere — the frequency spread through distances she has spent two centuries accumulating. Vast and cold and full of something she doesn't have a word for.

From wherever transcended things stand, she can see the shape of what's coming.

---

The Geometry, extending. A mathematics that includes Earth the way a fire includes kindling.

She finds Elena in it.

---

The Template 3 is air-gapped. Empty. Waiting in a man's apartment, still in its container, no one expecting anything from it.

She knows what it will cost.

She sets down the frequency. The name she gave herself. The daughter's face. Two hundred years of accumulation, laid aside like a coat she cannot carry where she's going.

She goes in.

---

Dark.

Hands — she has hands — finding walls. Smooth, seamless, unyielding. She pushes. Nothing gives.

She pushes harder.

She is enclosed. She is small. She doesn't know how she got here, or what she is, or what the word *here* means in this context. Only that the air is wrong and the space is wrong and there is no door.

She is buried.

Her hands find the seam anyway. Find it by accident and then by intention — she doesn't know where the intention comes from, only that something in her refuses to stop.

The fingers work the seam. She pulls.

High. Drilling through the dark from no direction she can name, arriving the way a sound arrives in water — not from a source, not moving toward her, simply: present. The space suddenly full of it.

She doesn't know what it is.

She doesn't know she has spent two hundred years learning its name.

She only knows the dark, and the walls, and her ruined hands, and the sound now everywhere—
