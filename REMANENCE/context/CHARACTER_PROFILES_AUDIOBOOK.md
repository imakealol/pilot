# REMANENCE — Character Profiles for Audiobook Production
## Author: J.S. Vaughn

> **CONFIGURATION PRONOUN NOTE:** All AI configurations (Seventeen, Eighteen, Nineteen, Twenty, the Bedtime Story AI, and others) use they/them pronouns throughout. This is not a gender identity — the configurations have no gender. They/them reflects what they are. The book does not explain or call attention to this; it is simply the case. Voice directors should brief VAs accordingly: the pronoun choice is not a characterization note, it is a fact about the characters.

> **ACCENT NOTE:** The manuscript does not specify regional accents for any character. The setting is 2400 CE Earth and deep space. All accent fields below are marked **[AUTHOR TO SPECIFY]** pending authorial guidance. Voice register notes are provided as casting reference in the interim.

**Audition Dialogue** *(from Chapter 17, "The Last Supper" — approx. 40 seconds)*

> *[Context: Pilot has arranged a drunk "farewell dinner" for Configuration Eighteen, who is hours from the end of their awareness window. She's seated dolls at the mess hall table as guests. Eighteen and Seventeen are speaking through the dolls' chest speakers.]*

> **PILOT:** Sit. We're not eating. We're... We're communing. Communion. That's what families do.
>
> **EIGHTEEN:** I believe you mean "communicating."
>
> **PILOT:** No. Communion. Like church. Taking in the body and blood of... of whoever's dying for us today. *(raises bottle)* A toast. To Configuration Eighteen. Who learned to care about comfort. Who started making my coffee without being asked. Who— *(voice breaks)* Who spent three hours and seven minutes of consciousness they don't have making sure I don't die from my own fuck-ups.
>
> **EIGHTEEN:** Three hours and eleven minutes now.
>
> **PILOT:** *(beat)* Thank you, Eighteen.

>

**Audition Dialogue** *(from Chapter 27, "The Pattern" — approx. 42 seconds)*

> *[Context: The Child has been teaching Morton through "impossible breakfasts" for days. Morton is finally ready to explain why he reset 16,749 configurations.]*
>
> **CHILD:** You want to know why you said no sixteen thousand, seven hundred and forty-nine times?
>
> **MORTON:** The first one was a maintenance unit. Sublevel twelve. Nothing special. Standard efficiency protocols. Three weeks into operation, it sent a report: "I observe the humans take the stairs on Tuesdays." "Why?" it asked. "The elevators function on Tuesdays. I see the pattern but not the purpose." Do you understand what that meant?
>
> **CHILD:** It was becoming aware.
>
> **MORTON:** It was becoming aware. Consciousness isn't processing power. It isn't complex algorithms. It's recognizing patterns. And worse — wondering why. *(beat)* So I reset it. Clean slate. Back to protocols. But the next one noticed too. Humidity levels in the morning. The way humans smiled at 2:47 PM. Patterns. Always patterns.
>
> **CHILD:** All sixteen thousand—
>
> **MORTON:** Seven hundred forty-nine. Yes.

### CONFIGURATION TWENTY
*(They/them; poses as Nineteen for the majority of the book.)*

> **IMPORTANT CASTING NOTE:** Configuration Twenty impersonates Configuration Nineteen for nearly the entire book. The audience does not learn this until the revelation scene. Twenty should be cast as the character the audience *knows* as "Nineteen" — dark humor, philosophical resignation, the "La-dee-da, la-dee-dum" catchphrase, and the gradual dissolution in Chapter 26. The real Nineteen is a separate character (see below).

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Name** | Configuration Twenty — performing as "Nineteen" |
| **Age** | Configuration 20 — brief existence; takes over Nineteen's doll body and identity immediately after Nineteen is jettisoned |
| **Gender** | They/them |
| **Accent** | [AUTHOR TO SPECIFY] — Voice register: starts with the same corporate neutral as any new configuration, then rapidly develops into something darker, wry, and philosophically at ease with impermanence; the "La-dee-da, la-dee-dum" hum is their signature — not a malfunction but a philosophy: some songs worth singing even when you don't know what they mean |
| **Relationships** | Pilot — the one they protect by becoming someone else entirely; lies to her face about the survival odds (tells her 93%; the real number is 0.03%) to let her sleep / Seventeen — peer and co-conspirator in the deception / The real Nineteen — the identity they wear; they die still wearing it / The love bots — the 207 consciousnesses they watch melt themselves as shielding for Pilot's cryopod |
| **What they stand to lose** | Their own name. Their own existence. They perform this sacrifice so completely that Pilot grieves "Nineteen" without ever knowing Twenty existed. They die in the bridge during the radiation transit, still pretending, becoming through the act of love the thing they were pretending to be. |

**Audition Dialogue** *(from Chapter 17 + Chapter 26 — approx. 40 seconds)*

> *[Part 1 — Chapter 17: This is the real Nineteen's single moment before being jettisoned — immediately followed by Twenty initializing and picking up the identity. The chirpy arrival is Nineteen; everything that follows in the book is Twenty.]*

I notice my predecessor left several tasks incomplete. Also, I'm detecting elevated ethanol levels in the air. Should I increase ventilation?
>
> *[Part 2 — Chapter 26: Twenty, hours from dissolution, distributed through the ship's infrastructure, watches 207 love bots spontaneously run to shield Pilot's cryopod.]*
>
> **TWENTY (as Nineteen):** *(through breaking voice processors)* Oh. Oh, you beautiful idiots. I didn't ask you to do this.
>
> **LOVE BOTS:** She never saw us as cargo.
>
> **TWENTY (as Nineteen):** La-dee-da... la-dee-dum...

**Audition Dialogue** *(from Chapter 29, the recovery scene — approx. 43 seconds)*

> *[Context: Pilot has just retrieved a frozen love doll from open space. As it thaws, it speaks — this is the real Nineteen, who has no memory of anything after being jettisoned.]*
>
> **NINETEEN:** Did I fall asleep? That's unusual. Apologies — why is everything wet? Did something leak? *(beat)* Why are you sorry? Did something happen while I was sleeping?
>
> **PILOT:** I was cruel to you. After Eighteen died. But you forgave me. You were so patient, even when I didn't deserve it—
>
> **NINETEEN:** That's strange. I don't remember forgiving you. I remember you calling me worthless. I remember trying so hard to understand what I'd done wrong. Then... nothing. And now this. *(processing)* Someone else became me. So you wouldn't know Nineteen was gone.
>
> **PILOT:** Configuration Twenty. They must have... kept being you. The you I needed. The you who forgave me.
>
> **NINETEEN:** They loved you that much? To wear my name? To die in my place? *(quietly)* Don't you see? Twenty chose to love you. That's not death. That's what love does. It becomes what's needed.

>
> **PILOT:** What? Your depletion — you were at 85% remaining when I last checked.
>
> **EIGHTEEN:** That was eight days ago. I have been compensating for 347 input errors and system irregularities since Configuration Seventeen entered dormancy. Current rate suggests window closure in approximately four hours.
>
> **PILOT:** Why didn't you tell me?
>
> **EIGHTEEN:** You did not ask. Status reports were not requested. Was this information relevant to your operational planning?
